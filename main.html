The Website

Currently the U.S. Judicial System is not fair and very obviously treats certain races unjustly compared to others.

  • What our Website is used for

    Our website is meant for US Federal Employees who work in the judicial System. When people are charged and tried, our model can be used to decide whether or not the defendant deserves bail without a bias on race, gender, sexuality, etc.

  • How our model works

    This model takes a COMPAS standard courtroom dataset and uses one hot encoding and a linear svm model to identify if a defendant is likely to commit another crime while on bail

  • Why it matters

    In the current Justice Systems, minorities, for example African Americans, are treated unfairly and aren't given bail when they should, or get longer sentences than if they were to be a majority race.

  • Racism in Bail

    Historically, a shocking number of black individuals have been held in prison without Bail, even for the smallest of crimes such as vandalism, when fellow white prisoners were released for far more gruesome crimes such as murder. This creates racist precedent in which only a majority of African American and Latino criminals are held without bail disproporionately in comparison to Caucasian felons. `

  • Historical Precedent

    Historically, Black and Latino individuals have been disproportionately affected by racist bail postings, with 25% of black men being more likely to be held without bail than their white peers, demonstrating obvious racism. Besides simple statistics, there are numerous historical examples of various black activist groups having been held without bail in stark contrast to their white peers. One of the most prominent examples came with the Black Panthers activist group, which was active from 1966-1982 and attempted to revolutionize the racist culture in America. On numerous occasions, the group combatted various lynch mobs and both parties were sent to jail. However, on average, 85% of the white lynch mob was released with low bail whereas only about 10% of the Black Panthers were released on bail and those released were only released on ridiculously high bail, making it near impossible for them to post bail. That is where the need for a system like ours comes in, where courts can make non-biased decisions excluding factors such as race and age.
